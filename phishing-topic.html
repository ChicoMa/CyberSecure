What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack. Social engineering attacks use psychological manipulation to trick a user into believing the interaction is with a trusted party. In the case of phishing, an email is sent containing either a link installing malware, to a fake website, or a fraudulent attachment. PDF's are the most common type of attachment found in a phishing email (2021 Tessian Report). Whereas phishing encompasses the broad approach of bulk emails sent to a series of contacts, spear phishing is personalised and targets an individual or organisation. Although typically conducted via emails, phishing can also happen through other communcation mediums like SMS messages (Smishing)



How Can You Spot a Phishing Email?

Although phishing emails can be hard to detect, there are still some very common signs which can indicate that an email is, in fact, not from a trusted organization;

  1. 1. Public email domain. A domain name is the address of a website and dictates the organization from which the email has come. An email ending in “@gmail.com” indicates that the email has come from a personal account.

  2. 2. Misspelt domain name. Domain names can be bought from a registrar and although they have to be unique, minor changes can make the address indistinguishable from the email being spoofed. For example, microsofrtonline.com.

  3. 3. Poorly written email. An email lacking official organisation logos or with conistently poor spelling and grammar, may indicate more than a typo error.

  4. 4. Suspicious links.

  5. 5. Sense of urgency. An email that expresses the importance of immediate action, may be attempting to trick a user into following through without thinking about legitamacy of the request.
